Scroll

Isaiah Buchberger researches culture, politics, and political economy — how collective political projects are held together, and come apart, through contingency and competing notions of the good.

A Change of Heart

An inquiry into how collective political projects are held together — and pulled apart — by contingency, and by the plural, often incommensurable notions of the good that animate them.

Unsettling political community

A comparative reading of involuntary celibates (incels) and contemporary Left organizations as case studies for breaking down conventional models of political community and belonging.

Isaiah is a graduate of the University of Chicago, where he received a B.A. in Anthropology with Honors and a Master's degree in Social Science.

On value

A long-term project, currently beginning, that addresses debates on value across symbolic anthropology, critical macro-finance, and cybernetics.

Presented widely

His work has been presented at multiple regional and national research conferences, and was supported by the Mellon Mays Undergraduate Fellowship.
