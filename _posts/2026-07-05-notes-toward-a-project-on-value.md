What do symbolic anthropology, critical macro-finance, and cybernetics have to say to one another about value? Each field has its own vocabulary for the question, and each tends to treat its own answer as exhaustive.

## The shape of the question

Anthropologists after Graeber have insisted that value is a social relation before it is a quantity. The macro-finance literature, meanwhile, treats value as an artifact of balance sheets and the hierarchies between them. Cybernetics offers a third frame: value as the output of a system regulating itself against its own measurements.

> The interesting problems begin where the frames disagree — where a balance sheet records something a community refuses to recognize, or a community holds something no ledger can carry.

A few threads this project will pull on:

- How incommensurable notions of the good survive inside nominally unified political projects
- What "liquidity" looks like from the standpoint of symbolic exchange
- Whether feedback is a metaphor or a mechanism in accounts of valuation
